WHY are these correct?
{% elif page == 2 %}

  • The ruler on the right is marked every 1 cm.
  • We can see that the object is between 8 cm and 9 cm.
    • We KNOW the first digit in our measurement must be an "8".
  • How much longer than 8 cm is the object? We can't say for sure.
    • We can GUESS a value after the decimal, like 8.2 cm or 8.3 cm.
  • Since the space between 8 and 9 cm is unmarked, adding more digits to the measurement just adds more guesses.
    • 8.2 cm contains one guess digit. 8.25 cm contains two.
  • By convention, a measurement can contain only ONE guess digit!

  • Both tools show the same overall range (0 g - 5 g).
  • The top tool has marks every 1 g. The bottom tool has marks every 0.1 g.
    • With more marks, the bottom tool places the guess digit further to the right.
    • 2.4 g for the top tool vs. 2.42 g for the bottom.
  • For the same range, adding more marks to the tool increses the precision of the measurement.

  • Both tools have the same number of markings.
  • The top tool has a range of 0 - 5 grams. The bottom tool has a smaller range (0 - 0.5 g).
    • With a smaller range, the bottom tool places the guess digit further to the right.
    • 3.48 g for the top tool vs. 0.348 g for the bottom.
  • For the same number of tick marks, a smaller range increses the precision of the measurement.

Some frequent data-recording errors.

Trailing zeros DO serve a purpose!

True or False:

The measurements 4 cm, 4.0 cm, and 4.00 cm mean the same thing.

FALSE!

    Different rulers were used to take each measurement.
  • 4 cm: The ruler was marked every 10 cm.
  • 4.0 cm: The ruler was marked every 1 cm.
  • 4.00 cm: The ruler was marked every 0.1 cm.
